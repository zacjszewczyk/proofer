Fog index: {FOG_INDEX:.2f}↓The Gunning Fog Index estimates the years of formal education needed to understand the text on a first reading. To lower the fog index: increase sentence count (higher sentence_count leads to a smaller ratio of word count/sentence count); decrease complex words (reducing the number of complex words lowers the ratio of complex words/word count); maintain sentence length (avoid excessively short sentences, which might artificially inflate the sentence count without simplifying the text); and simplify vocabulary (replace complex words with simpler synonyms.)


Reading ease: {READING_EASE}↑Flesch–Kincaid reading ease: higher scores indicate material that is easier to read; lower numbers indicate difficulty. To improve, reduce the number of words per sentence by using shorter sentences. Introduce more sentence breaks (periods, question marks, exclamation marks). Also, Use words with fewer syllables. Replace complex words with simpler synonyms.


Grade level: {GRADE_LEVEL:.2f}↓Flesch–Kincaid grade level: estimates the number of years of education generally required to understand this text. To optimize, decrease word count/sentence count ratio. Use more, shorter sentences: break up long sentences into multiple shorter ones. Decrease word count: prefer concise writing and use fewer words overall. Decrease syllable count/word count ratio by using more simpler words with fewer syllables. Avoid using multi-syllable words unnecessarily.
